# BrainFlow MVP — Product Requirements Document

## Vision & goal
BrainFlow integrates science-based micro-exercises into the daily lives of people in a
distraction-heavy world so they can think more clearly, work more focused, and live more
balanced. Strategic goal: reach **2,500 monthly active users in the DACH region by Q4 2026**,
with at least 30% practicing micro-exercises as a daily habit (min. 4x per week).

## Target user
Working adults aged 25–45 in a stressful, distraction-heavy work environment who want to
improve their mental health but have little time or energy for elaborate routines. Primary
persona: **Jonas Weber, 38**, software developer in Munich, works fully remote, high tech
affinity, **uses an Android smartphone**. Has tried 3 meditation apps and abandoned each
after 2 weeks because long sessions and complex UIs put him off.

## MVP scope (the core value: daily guided brain training in a few minutes)
1. **Onboarding** with a frictionless entry ("Start Today").
2. **Guided exercise** screen with a timer and instructions. **Exercises last 2–5 minutes.**
3. **Basic tracking**: streak + completed sessions.
4. Four training categories surfaced as tiles: Focus, Creativity, Emotional IQ, Wellbeing.

## MoSCoW prioritisation
- **Must have:** Onboarding + Start Today; guided exercises; basic tracking (streaks).
- **Should have:** category selection; progress-over-time view.
- **Could have:** reminders / notifications.
- **Won't have (this MVP):** Premium content, **AI-powered Daily Picks**, and monetisation.
  These are explicitly out of scope for the MVP.

## Monetisation timing
The premium upgrade prompt appears on **Day 7** after first use (after the user has
experienced their first streak). Rationale: establish trust *before* asking for payment.

## B2B reporting requirement
The B2B HR dashboard exposes **anonymised, aggregated usage data only**. No individual
employee-level engagement data is shown to employers, by design.

## Success metric
Primary product KPI: **30-day retention ≥ 40%** (warning threshold < 25%), measured weekly.
